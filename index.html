Nelson Mandela

The timeline of Nelson Mandela

  • 1918: Born into the Madiba clan in the village of Mvezo, in the Eastern Cape
  • Childhood: His mother was Nonqaphi Nosekeni and his father was Nkosi Mphakanyiswa Gadla Mandela, principal counsellor to the Acting King of the Thembu people, Jongintaba Dalindyebo.
  • 1930: He was 12 years old, his father died and the young Rolihlahla became a ward of Jongintaba at the Great Place in Mqhekezweni1.
  • School: He attended primary school in Qunu where his teacher, Miss Mdingane, gave him the name Nelson, in accordance with the custom of giving all schoolchildren “Christian” names.
  • School: He completed his Junior Certificate at Clarkebury Boarding Institute and went on to Healdtown, a Wesleyan secondary school of some repute, where he matriculated.
  • University: Mandela began his studies for a Bachelor of Arts degree at the University College of Fort Hare but did not complete the degree there as he was expelled for joining in a student protest.
  • 1941: There he worked as a mine security officer and after meeting Walter Sisulu, an estate agent.
  • 1943: He completed his BA through the University of South Africa and went back to Fort Hare for his graduation in 1943
  • 1944: married Walter Sisulu’s cousin, Evelyn Mase. And joined the African National Congress in 1944 when he helped to form the ANC Youth League (ANCYL)
  • 1952: He was chosen as the National Volunteer-in-Chief of the Defiance Campaign with Maulvi Cachalia as his deputy
  • 1961: he was asked to lead the armed struggle and helped to establish Umkhonto weSizwe (Spear of the Nation), which launched on 16 December 1961 with a series of explosions.
  • 1964: Mandela and seven other accused, Walter Sisulu, Ahmed Kathrada, Govan Mbeki, Raymond Mhlaba, Denis Goldberg, Elias Motsoaledi and Andrew Mlangeni, were convicted and the next day were sentenced to life imprisonment.
  • 1990: Released from prison after 27 years of imprisonment
  • 1994: Became the first president of South Africa and ruled till 1999

  • 2013: He died at his home in Johannesburg on 5 December 2013.
